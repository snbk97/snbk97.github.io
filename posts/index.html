There are around 9 mental models mentioned in the The Great Mental Models book. I'm going to describe last but not the least resonating mental model of them, which is know as Occam's Razor. [Read more] Occam's Razor is a principle, which state that - Suppose there exist two explanations for an occurrence. In this case the one that requires the least speculation is usually correct. Another way of saying it is that the more assumptions you have to make, the more unlikely an explanation.

It's fast, easy and instant. No need to scratch you head to think about the future.
