There are around 9 mental model mentioned in the TGMM book. I’m going to describe last but not the least resonating mental model of them, which is know as Occam’s Razor. [Read more] Occam’s Razor is a principle, which state that - Suppose there exist two explanations for an occurrence. In this case the one that requires the least speculation is usually correct. Another way of saying it is that the more assumptions you have to make, the more unlikely an explanation.

#tgmm  #review 
Continuing with the previous [post], I want to talk about the next mental model that is know as Second-order thinking[Read More] This mental model enables us to evaluate our decision that we take to solve our problems for the time being and not worry about it’s consequence in the long run. It’s fast, easy and instant. No need to scratch you head to think about the future. On the contrary, Second Order thinking need a little time to think and consider the consequence which you might face in the long term because of this one decision you make.
