The Great Mental Models - 2

2019-07-24 :: 2 min read
#tgmm  #review 

Continuing with the previous [post], I want to talk about the next mental model that is know as Second-order thinking[Read More]

This mental model enables us to evaluate our decision that we take to solve our problems for the time being and not worry about it's consequence in the long run. It's fast, easy and instant. No need to scratch you head to think about the future.

On the contrary, Second Order thinking need a little time to think and consider the consequence which you might face in the long term because of this one decision you make. Sometime it's not enough to think to the second order, we can choose to think even one step further to the Third Order of thinking. The decisions we make must have an answer to “Why?” we are taking this decision in every step of the way. Is it valuable in the long run.

A excellent example given in the book was:

Adding more traction to car tires, makes it easier to control on the roads and slides less. (This is an example of first order thinking)

But the second order thinking comes into play if we think about the effects it's going to have on the environment and the car parts.

Due to more traction, the engine now has to work harder, resulting in less mileage of the vehicle. Also this cause more emission in the environment and more rubber particles left out in the ground. (Second order thinking)
